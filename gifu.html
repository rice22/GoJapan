Shirakawa-go Village

Whether blanketed in snow or glowing at dusk, Shirakawa-go radiates rustic charm, heritage, and harmony with nature.

Shirakawa-go (白川郷, literally “White River Village”) is a picturesque, historic mountain village nestled in the remote Shogawa River Valley of Gifu Prefecture in central Japan. Along with its neighboring village Gokayama in Toyama Prefecture, Shirakawa-go is famous for its traditional gasshō-zukuri (合掌造り) farmhouses, some of which are over 250 years old. In 1995, the area was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site for its outstanding preservation of architectural and cultural heritage in a natural setting.

Visiting Shirakawa-go

Shirakawa-go transforms with the seasons, offering breathtaking beauty year-round—from vibrant cherry blossoms in spring and lush green paddies in summer to fiery autumn foliage and a magical winter blanket of snow that highlights the iconic steep thatched roofs, creating a picturesque, postcard-perfect village scene.

Visitors are encouraged to be respectful: don’t enter private property, keep noise levels down, and avoid drone usage without permission.

Practical Information

  • Opening Hours:Village accessible 24/7, but museums and homes open between 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM
  • Admission:
  • Village entrance is free.
  • Wada House: 400 yen
  • Kanda House: 300 yen
  • Gassho-zukuri Minka-en: 600 yen
  • Address: Ogimachi, Shirakawa Village, Ōno District, Gifu 501-5627, Japan
  • Access: Shirakawa-go is located in Gifu Prefecture and is most easily accessed by bus, as there is no train station in the village itself.
  • From Takayama: Nohi Bus (50 minutes, ~2,600 yen round trip)
  • From Kanazawa: Hokuriku Railroad/Kaetsuno Bus (75 minutes, ~3,600 yen round trip)
  • From Nagoya: Direct highway buses take ~2.5 hours
  • Gassho-zukuri Minka-en: 600 yen

Shirakawa-go Travel Tips

Getting There & Around

  • Plan Ahead for Transportation: The village is accessible by bus from nearby cities like Takayama, Kanazawa, and Nagoya—check schedules in advance, especially in peak seasons.
  • Use Shuttle Buses or Walk: Shirakawa-go is compact and best explored on foot; shuttle buses connect key spots if you prefer not to walk long distances.
  • Consider Staying Overnight: Many farmhouses operate as guesthouses (minshuku), offering a unique immersive experience beyond the day trip.
  • Currency Exchange & ATM Services: For more freedom and to explore surrounding areas, bicycle rentals are available but be prepared for hilly terrain.

Cultural Etiquette & Practical Tips

  • Respect Local Customs: Many houses are private homes or guesthouses; always follow signage and ask before taking photos indoors.
  • Prepare for Weather: Pack layers and waterproof footwear, as weather can change quickly, especially in winter.
  • Bring Cash: While some shops accept cards, many small businesses and guesthouses operate on cash-only.
  • Learn Basic Japanese Phrases: Simple greetings and thank-yous go a long way in this traditional village setting.

Visitor Information & Tips

  • Try Local Delicacies: Don’t miss regional specialties like mountain vegetables, Hida beef, and soba noodles served in traditional settings.
  • Sample Doburoku Sake: Visit the Doburoku Festival Museum or local breweries to taste this unique, unfiltered rice wine native to the region.
  • Visit Local Markets: Explore small markets for handmade crafts, fresh produce, and souvenirs that reflect the village’s culture.
  • Join Seasonal Festivals: Time your visit to experience local events like the Doburoku Festival in October or winter light-ups for a deeper cultural connection.
