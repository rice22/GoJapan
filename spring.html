🎯 Spring Trivia

What is the Japanese term for cherry blossom viewing?

Hanami

Literally meaning "flower viewing"

How long do cherry blossoms typically bloom?

1-2 weeks

Peak bloom lasts only a few days

Which city is famous for the Philosopher's Path?

Kyoto

A peaceful walking path lined with hundreds of cherry trees

What do people traditionally eat during hanami?

Bento & Sake

Picnic boxes and traditional rice wine

When does cherry blossom season start in Tokyo?

Late March

Usually around March 20-25

How many varieties of cherry trees are there in Japan?

Over 200

Somei Yoshino is the most common

What is the cherry blossom forecast called?

Sakura Zensen

Cherry blossom front - tracks bloom progression

What does sakura symbolize in Japanese culture?

Life's Beauty

The fleeting nature of life and beauty

🗺️ Your Spring Adventure Timeline

Early Morning (6:00 AM)

🌅 Sunrise Photography

Capture the golden hour light filtering through cherry blossoms. Popular spots include Chidorigafuchi and Ueno Park.

Morning (9:00 AM)

🏛️ Temple & Garden Visits

Explore traditional temples surrounded by sakura. Visit Senso-ji Temple or Kiyomizu-dera for spiritual moments among the blossoms.

Afternoon (12:00 PM)

🍱 Hanami Picnic

Join locals for traditional hanami picnics. Bring bento boxes, sake, and enjoy the festive atmosphere under blooming trees.

Late Afternoon (3:00 PM)

🚶‍♀️ Scenic Walks

Stroll along the Philosopher's Path in Kyoto or around the Imperial Palace East Gardens for peaceful cherry blossom viewing.

Evening (6:00 PM)

🌸 Yozakura (Night Viewing)

Experience illuminated cherry blossoms at night. Many parks offer special lighting during sakura season for magical evening views.

Night (8:00 PM)

🍜 Spring Dining

Enjoy seasonal kaiseki cuisine featuring spring ingredients like bamboo shoots, cherry blossom mochi, and sakura tea.
