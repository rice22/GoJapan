Rise with the Majesty of Shizuoka’s Mt. Fuji

A breathtaking gateway to Japan’s natural and cultural treasures, Shizuoka surrounds Mt. Fuji with serene tea fields, historic temples, and lively local markets. From tranquil mountain views to fresh seafood delights, every moment invites discovery. Whether you’re hiking Fuji’s trails or savoring Shizuoka’s flavors, this region pulses with timeless beauty and vibrant tradition.

Mount Fuji (富士山, Fujisan), located on the border between Shizuoka and Yamanashi prefectures, is Japan's tallest and most iconic mountain, rising to 3,776 meters (12,389 feet). Revered for centuries as a sacred site and celebrated in countless works of art, Mount Fuji is a symbol of Japan’s natural beauty, cultural heritage, and spiritual identity. The mountain and its surrounding areas, including the Shizuoka side, were designated a UNESCO World Heritage Cultural Site in 2013 under the title "Fujisan, sacred place and source of artistic inspiration."

Visiting Mount Fuji

Mt. Fuji, Japan’s iconic sacred mountain, rises majestically above the surrounding landscapes of Shizuoka and Yamanashi prefectures. More than just a natural wonder, it is a cultural symbol woven into the fabric of Japanese art, religion, and tradition. Whether you’re here to hike its trails, explore nearby lakes, or experience local cuisine and customs, Mt. Fuji offers an unforgettable journey into the heart of Japan’s spirit and beauty.

Where Nature Meets Legend — Experience the Timeless Majesty and Cultural Soul of Japan’s Sacred Mountain.

Practical Information

  • Main Facilities
  • Fujisan World Heritage Centre: 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM (closed Tuesdays)
  • Fujinomiya 5th Station: Open during climbing season only (early July to early Sept)
  • Shiraito Falls: Open year-round; no admission fee
  • Miho no Matsubara: Open year-round
  • Climbing Essentials:
  • Proper gear is essential (layered clothing, headlamp, gloves).
  • Altitude sickness can affect climbers. Consider overnight huts (book in advance).
  • Bring cash, as many mountain huts and shops do not accept credit cards.
  • Admission:
  • Viewing is free; some facilities like the Fujisan World Heritage Centre charge a small fee (approx. 300–500 yen).
  • A voluntary climbing fee (1,000 yen) is requested during the climbing season to support trail maintenance.
  • Access:
  • From Tokyo:
    • Take the Tokaido Shinkansen to Shizuoka or Shin-Fuji Station (about 1–1.5 hours).
    • From Shin-Fuji, local buses connect to Fujinomiya, Shiraito Falls, and the 5th Station.
  • From Osaka/Kyoto:
    • Take the Tokaido Shinkansen to Shizuoka (about 2–3 hours), then transfer to local transport.

Mount Fuji Travel Tips

Climbing & Outdoor Preparation

  • Choose Your Trail Wisely: There are four main trails (Yoshida, Subashiri, Gotemba, and Fujinomiya), each offering different levels of difficulty, scenery, and amenities.
  • Time It Right: The official climbing season is from early July to early September. Outside this period, the trails are closed or unsafe due to snow and harsh weather.
  • Pack for Altitude: Even in summer, temperatures near the summit can be close to freezing. Bring layers, gloves, and rain gear.
  • Acclimate & Rest: Altitude sickness is common. Stay a night at the 5th Station or hike slowly to adjust before reaching the summit.

Sightseeing & Scenic Spots

  • Visit the Fuji Five Lakes: Explore Kawaguchiko, Saiko, Yamanakako, Shojiko, and Motosuko for incredible views, boat rides, and hot springs.
  • Check the Weather First: Mt. Fuji is often hidden behind clouds. Early mornings (before 9 AM) and winter months offer the clearest views.
  • Stop by Chureito Pagoda: One of the most iconic photo spots with a panoramic view of Mt. Fuji framed by cherry blossoms or autumn foliage.
  • Ride the Mt. Fuji Panoramic Ropeway: A quick gondola ride near Lake Kawaguchi offers a sweeping vista of Mt. Fuji and the surrounding area.

Culture & Local Experiences

  • Try Fujinomiya Yakisoba: This local stir-fried noodle dish is chewy, savory, and topped with crunchy sardine flakes—a specialty of the Mt. Fuji foothills.
  • Visit the Mt. Fuji World Heritage Centre: This immersive museum in Fujinomiya combines interactive exhibits with striking architecture inspired by the mountain.
  • Soak in a Fuji-view Onsen: Relax in a hot spring bath with a clear view of the mountain—great options are available in Hakone, Kawaguchiko, and Yamanakako.
  • Join a Tea Farm Tour: The Shizuoka region offers green tea experiences where you can pick, brew, and taste tea with Mt. Fuji as your scenic backdrop.
