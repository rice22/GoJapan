🎯 Summer Trivia

What is Japan's most famous summer fireworks display?

Sumida River Fireworks

Tokyo's iconic hanabi event dating back to the Edo period

What traditional clothing is worn during summer festivals?

Yukata

A lightweight cotton kimono for hot weather

Which festival is known for colorful streamers in August?

Tanabata Festival

Celebrates the star-crossed lovers Orihime and Hikoboshi

What sweet treat is popular in Japanese summers?

Kakigōri

Shaved ice topped with syrup and condensed milk

What dance is performed during Obon?

Bon Odori

A folk dance to welcome ancestral spirits

What insect is a symbol of summer in Japan?

Cicada

Their loud songs define the season

Where is the Awa Odori dance festival held?

Tokushima

One of Japan’s largest traditional dance festivals

What is the purpose of hanging wind chimes in summer?

To invite coolness

The sound is believed to evoke a sense of breeze

🗺️ Your Summer Adventure Timeline

Early Morning (6:00 AM)

🌅 Coastal Sunrise Walk

Start your day with a peaceful stroll along Enoshima Beach or the Izu Peninsula as the sun rises over the Pacific.

Morning (9:00 AM)

🎋 Tanabata Decorations & Temple Visits

Visit temples like Zojo-ji or Asakusa adorned with colorful Tanabata streamers and paper wishes tied to bamboo.

Afternoon (12:00 PM)

🍧 Street Food & Kakigōri Break

Explore a local summer matsuri. Try yakisoba, grilled squid, and cool down with flavored shaved ice under lantern canopies.

Late Afternoon (3:00 PM)

👘 Yukata Stroll & Photo Spots

Dress in a traditional yukata and walk through scenic spots like Kamakura or Gion, capturing seasonal charm.

Evening (6:00 PM)

🎆 Fireworks by the River

Watch fireworks (hanabi) over rivers like the Sumida or Osaka rivers, a quintessential Japanese summer experience.

Night (8:00 PM)

🕯️ Bon Odori & Lantern Festivals

Join locals in Bon dances at shrines, or watch floating lanterns drift along rivers to honor ancestors.
