Matsushima Bay (松島湾, Matsushima-wan), located on the eastern coast of Miyagi Prefecture near Sendai, is renowned for its ethereal beauty and is celebrated as one of Japan’s Nihon Sankei (Three Most Scenic Views), alongside Amanohashidate in Kyoto and Miyajima in Hiroshima. The bay is dotted with over 260 pine-covered islets, creating a serene and almost dreamlike seascape that has inspired poets, painters, and pilgrims for centuries.

Visiting Matsushima Bay

Matsushima Bay, located in Miyagi Prefecture, is a breathtaking natural wonder dotted with over 260 pine-covered islets. Celebrated as one of Japan’s Three Scenic Views, it offers a serene blend of coastal beauty, historical sites, and traditional culture. Whether you're cruising the bay, strolling temple grounds, or enjoying fresh seafood, Matsushima provides a timeless escape into Japan’s scenic soul.

Matsushima is a year-round destination, but spring and autumn offer the most photogenic scenes.

Practical Information

  • Godaido Hall
  • Hours: Open 24/7 (no admission fee, best viewed during daylight)
  • Note: Be cautious crossing the arched bridges, especially during wet weather.
  • Zuiganji Temple
  • Hours: 8:00 AM to 3:30 PM (subject to seasonal changes)
  • Admission: 700 yen (adults), includes entrance to the museum
  • Address: 91 Chonai, Matsushima, Miyagi 981-0213
  • Matsushima Bay Cruise
  • Hours: 9:00 AM – 4:00 PM (varies by season and weather)
  • Duration: 50 minutes
  • Fare: ~1,500 yen (adults), discounts for round-trips or multiple riders
  • Boarding Point: Near Matsushima-Kaigan Station
  • Access:
  • Matsushima is easily accessible from Sendai, the capital of Miyagi Prefecture.
  • From Sendai Station: Take the JR Senseki Line to Matsushima-Kaigan Station (approximately 40 minutes)
  • From Tokyo: Take the Tohoku Shinkansen to Sendai Station (~1.5 hours), then transfer to the Senseki Line
  • Local buses and rental bikes are available around the area for further exploration.

Matsushima Bay Travel Tips

Getting Around

  • Ferry Tours: Take a scenic ferry ride to explore the islands up close and enjoy panoramic views of the bay.
  • Bicycle Rentals: Rent a bike to cycle along the coastal paths for a leisurely and flexible way to see the area.
  • Walking Trails: Explore the well-marked walking trails such as the Fukuura Island trail for peaceful nature immersion.
  • Local Bus Services: Use local buses connecting Matsushima with nearby towns for convenient travel without a car.

What to Bring

  • Comfortable Footwear: Wear sturdy shoes for walking uneven terrain and trails around the islands.
  • Weather-Appropriate Clothing: Layered clothing is best, as Matsushima’s weather can change quickly, especially near the water.
  • Camera or Smartphone: Capture stunning views of pine-covered islands, seasonal foliage, and sunrise/sunset scenes.
  • Cash: Many small shops and eateries may not accept credit cards, so keep some cash handy.

Cultural Etiquette & Safety

  • Respect Nature: Avoid littering and stay on designated paths to protect the delicate ecosystem.
  • Quiet Zones: Maintain a respectful volume in temples and shrines, such as Zuiganji Temple, to preserve the tranquil atmosphere.
  • Photography Guidelines: Ask permission before photographing locals or religious ceremonies.
  • Emergency Contacts: Keep local emergency numbers saved and know the location of the nearest tourist information center.
