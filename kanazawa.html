Kenrokuen Garden (兼六園, Kenroku-en), located in Kanazawa, Ishikawa Prefecture, is one of Japan’s most celebrated landscape gardens and has earned its place as one of the “Three Great Gardens of Japan,” alongside Korakuen in Okayama and Kairakuen in Mito. The name "Kenrokuen" means “Garden of the Six Sublimities,” referring to the six essential attributes of a perfect landscape garden as defined in Chinese tradition: spaciousness, seclusion, artificiality, antiquity, abundant water, and broad views. Kenrokuen uniquely harmonizes all six—an exceptionally rare achievement.

Visiting Kenrokuen Garden

Kenrokuen Garden is more than just a historic landscape—it's a living expression of Japan’s seasonal beauty, harmony, and timeless design. Whether you seek reflection, inspiration, or simply a scenic stroll, this iconic garden invites you to slow down and savor nature at its finest. As one of the Three Great Gardens of Japan, it offers a rich cultural and sensory experience that evolves with the passing months.

Kenrokuen: Where every season paints a masterpiece.

Practical Information

  • Opening Hours:
  • March 1 – October 15: 7:00 AM to 6:00 PM
  • October 16 – end of February: 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM
  • Admission:
  • Adults: 320 yen
  • Children (6–17): 100 yen
  • Free entry on selected national holidays (e.g., Culture Day, November 3)
  • Address: 1-1 Marunouchi, Kanazawa, Ishikawa Prefecture 920-0937, Japan
  • Access:
  • From Kanazawa Station, take the Kanazawa Loop Bus (Left Loop) and get off at Kenrokuen-shita or Hirosaka bus stop (approx. 15-minute ride).
  • Alternatively, a 30-minute walk or 10-minute taxi ride from Kanazawa Station.

Kenrokuen Garden Travel Tips

Best Times to Visit

  • Spring Blossoms: Visit late March to early April to enjoy the iconic cherry blossoms in full bloom, lighting up the garden with soft pink hues.
  • Autumn Foliage: Late October to early November offers stunning red and gold maple leaves, perfect for photography and peaceful strolls.
  • Winter Yukitsuri: December to February features the famous yukitsuri ropes protecting the trees from snow, creating a unique winter wonderland atmosphere.
  • Early Morning Visits: Arrive early in the morning to avoid crowds and experience a tranquil atmosphere with softer light for photos.

Practical Tips for Visitors

  • Wear Comfortable Shoes: The garden has uneven paths and gentle hills; sturdy shoes will make your visit more enjoyable.
  • Bring a Camera: Kenrokuen’s scenic views and seasonal changes provide countless photo opportunities, so don’t forget your camera or smartphone.
  • Stay Hydrated: Especially in warmer months, carry water with you as there are limited refreshment stalls inside the garden.
  • Respect the Rules: Follow signs and stay on designated paths to protect the delicate plants and maintain the garden’s beauty.

Visitor Information & Tips

  • Guided Tours: Join a guided tour to learn about the garden’s history, design, and hidden features not easily noticed on your own.
  • Visit Nearby Attractions: Explore Seisonkaku Villa and Kanazawa Castle nearby to deepen your understanding of the region’s culture.
  • Enjoy a Tea Break: Stop by the garden’s tea house to relax with traditional matcha tea and sweets while soaking in the surroundings.
  • Seasonal Festivals: Check for special events or light-up festivals, particularly in spring and winter, to see the garden in a magical setting.
